my writing

a medley of 2 a.m. thoughts

2:59 am

sydney's voice drifts from a steady narration to silence,
my phone telling me that it's time to try and sleep,
fifteen minutes have passed.
my eyelids, once filled with sydney's story, now flutter open
my feathery pillowcase cushioning the landing into this world.
thoughts, worries, questions, gratitude
from yesterday, about tomorrow,
cloud my mind like a stream of bubbles,
i'm unsure which ones to pop, which ones to save.
or maybe i should just let them all rise,
knowing they will manifest in their own time.
i shift my alarm back another fifteen
wishful thinking that the next time i am awake,
it will not be 3:59 am.
